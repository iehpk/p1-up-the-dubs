Attractions

Make your Dublin experience unforgettable by paying a visit to these great attractions around the city, which are bound to live a lasting impression.

1

Guinness Storehouse Official Site

There are many mysteries of Ireland, the tastier of which is the mysterious Black beer that is Guinness.

Get to know all about this myserious Guinness, who made it, when was it made, how is it made, and best of all, get a taste for free from the source itself and while at it, why not learn how to pour the best pint for yourself? There might be a certificate in it for you!

How To Get There

From our baseline, that is the Dublin Spire, transportation is aplent.

  1. Ride the Dublin Bike from O'Connell Street to the Store. There is a station next to the Visitor's center.
  2. Grab 1 of many buses, the best of which is route 123, which goes from Top of O'Connell, right to the front door.
  3. Get on the red line Luas from the Abbey stop to either Heuston or St. Jame's.
  4. Take one of the three Hop On / Hop Off buses that will also leave you at the store entrance.
  5. FInally, you can walk, if dedicated - a journey that will take you about 40 minutes from O'Connell Street.

Admission

Tickets are necessarry to get admission to the Guiness Storehouse. There are different tickets for different experiences.

Opening Hours: 09:30 - 16:45

2

St. Patrick's CathedralOfficial Site

So, you didn't know that there are no snakes in Ireland! Well, there isn't. The legend goes, it's thanks to St. Patrick - the Apotsle of Ireland.

While it is ideal to include March 17th in your holiday plans to Ireland in order to join the celebrations of St. Patrick, the cathedral is open beyond just St. Patrick's Day. It is a visiting sight. Sometimes it is off limits due to Graduations.

How To Get There

Transportation to St. Patrick's Cathedral is a bit tricky but it is also much closer to our baseline.

  1. Due to its location, walking is the best option. Many sites are on the way so the walk won't be a waste of time.
  2. Bus options are available but they have to be taken from across the river from O'Connell St. Route 123 can leave you a bit close though.
  3. The Dublin Bikes are also available to you. There is a station outside the Cathedral.
  4. Hop On / Hop Off buses are also an option and will drop you off outside.

Admission

Tickets are necessarry to get inside

Opening Hours: 09:00 - 17:00

3

Temple Bar Area The Pub Village

Ireland is the country of drinkers it is no secret, so why not visit the place that was named after drinker's place in Temple Bar?

It might be super popular, hence the pricey cost but it is worth the cash. Filled with nightly pubs and restaurants that have live musicians playing to sooth your food down, in addition to plenty of vintage shops, don't miss this opportunity to dine be down with the Lads.

How To Get There

Situated just across the pond from O'Connell St, it is a mere walk of hand-countable minutes.

  1. Due to its location, walking is the best option. Enjoy the many artistic bridges on the way.
  2. Dublin Bikes are still available to you.
  3. Hop On / Hop Off buses are also an option and will drop you off on Dame Street, the other face of Temple Bar.

Admission

Open area. Bookings might be necessary for some Restaurants.

Opening Hours: Regular Restaurant and Pub opening hours.

4

The Book Of Kells & Trinity College Official Site

Like is not all about having fun. For all the scholars out there, you are covered in the form of the oldest book in the world - The Book Of Kells!

Located within the prestigous library in the equally prestigous University - Trinity College, why not feast your eyes on this legendary scripture?

How To Get There

Being in the middle of the city, getting to Trinity College could not be any easier.

  1. Due to its location, walking is the best option.
  2. Bus options are also really good, with virtually all routes passing by the Trinity area.
  3. The Dublin Bikes are also available to you. There are stations outside and around the College.
  4. Hop On / Hop Off buses are also an option and will drop you off outside.

Admission

Tickets are necessarry to see The Book of Kells but not to enter the College.

Opening Hours: 08:30 - 18:00

5

Pheonix Park A Park so large it houses the Dublin Zoo

Some people just want to lay low and wind down or get in touch with their inner selves while bonding with nature.

There is the best place for self time. So wide and vast you will have all the privacy you need. And if you want to extend your senses to the wild life, then the zoom will be just a curiosity away wtihin the same park.

How To Get There

Being at the edge of the city and given its size, getting to Pheonix Park might seem daunting but it is actually easy to get to.

  1. Dublin Bikes are still available to you.
  2. Hop On / Hop Off buses go to Pheonix Park as well so you can take them there.
  3. The red line Luas passes close to the southside of the park. Just get off on either the Museum stop or at Hesuton station.
  4. Bus route 46A from upper O'Connell Street can drop you off oppsite the entrance to the Zoo at the top on the park.
  5. While it is at the edge of the city, you can still walk to the park enjoying the view as you go.

Admission

Open area. Tickets are necessary to get access to the Zoo.

Opening Hours: 09:30 - 18:00 for the Zoo. 24hr for the park.
